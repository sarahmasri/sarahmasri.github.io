About

A litte
get to
know me.

I'm a data analyst and statistician based in Vancouver, BC. My work sits at the intersection of rigorous quantitative modelling and the kind of clear communication that actually gets used — by decision-makers, collaborators, and teams across disciplines.

My academic background is in mathematics and statistics at UBC, where I completed an MSc focused on epidemiological modelling — specifically formalising the equivalence between stochastic compartmental models (SIR/SEIR) and the Hawkes process as a unified mathematical framework.

Today at Creative Destruction Lab Vancouver, I manage data infrastructure and build reporting workflows that support both operational decisions and venture-level strategy across multiple programme streams.

I care about building systems that are not just technically correct, but genuinely usable — and about making complex ideas legible to the people who need to act on them.
